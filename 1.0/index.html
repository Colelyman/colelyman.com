Walmart Sold MP3s?

Apparently Walmart did sell MP3s, for seven years too. Walmart opened its MP3 store seven years ago to try to compete with iTunes, but obviously did not do as well.

Walmart did have the "lowest price", 88 cents for a single track compared to 99 cents in iTunes (back in 2003). They did have restrictions on how many devices the track could be put on and how many times it could be copied.

The MP3 store is officially closing at the end of August. If you were a walmart MP3 customer they will still offer support for your music.

Linux, Government, and You

The Department of Defense and Air Force Research Laboratory have recently realized the advantages of linux. They designed their own linux distrobution that is totally sandboxed. Sandboxed basically means that any person using this linux distribution has the ability to remain totally anaymous and seperate from the computer that they are using. This given them the oppurtunity to use unsecure computers anywhere, and still remain virtually safe.

Linux is perfect for this kind of covert computing because linux is portable and secure. When I say that linux is portable, I mean that you can run linux from a flash drive or from a CD. Which means that you can literally take your whole computer in your pocket. This ability of portablility that linux has also makes it more secure in these covert situations. When using a thumb drive or CD on an unsecure computer, you leave no trace that you even used the computer because the operating system is run totally from the thumb drive or CD.

What good does this linux distribution do to you? Well this distribution of linux can give you the oppurtunity to use an unsecured computer for secure internet browsing,file transfers, or what ever you can dream up. You can find the distibuiton on this website http://spi.dod.mil/lipose.htm. There is also a PDF Guide to help you get started if you want secure computing.
